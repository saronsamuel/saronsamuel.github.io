A poem about my love of all pasta... except spaghetti




Pasta, my love, from the very start,\
You fill my bowl and warm my heart.\
Seconds and thirds, I'll gladly take,\
But spaghetti? That’s a mistake.

My mom’s lasagna, best by far,\
Rich and comforting, a culinary star.\
A hearty meal that can't be beat,\
While spaghetti’s just tangled wheat.

Penne, with ridges, firm and right,\
You hold the sauce with all your might.\
Each forkful’s joy, it never fails,\
But spaghetti flops, like wet noodles’ tales.

Rigatoni, big and strong,\
In you, no sauce is ever wrong.\
A sturdy bite that keeps its form,\
Unlike spaghetti, a limp, sad norm.

Gemelli twists and fusilli swirls,\
Both pasta shapes that bring me twirls.\
Their playful forms just can't go wrong,\
While spaghetti stretches far too long.

Tagliatelle, smooth and fine,\
Your elegance is truly divine.\
You keep your place upon my plate,\
While spaghetti’s mess seals its fate.

Macaroni, you raised me up,\
Through every bowl and every cup.\
You’re comfort food, a lifelong friend,\
While spaghetti? You didn’t pitch in ’til the end.

So here I stand, with fork in hand,\
For pasta, my true love grand.\
Spaghetti may have fans galore,\
But in my heart, you’re out the door.
