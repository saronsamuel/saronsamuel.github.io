  1. Video Event Retrieval:

    Developed a multilingual video-based event retrieval framework. Trained XLM-R for task-specific reranking and integrated state-of-the-art ASR and OCR model rankings to enhance cross-modal retrieval across diverse video content. Overall, achieved an improvement of around 20-points for nDCG@10.

  2. Prompts as Auto-Optimized Training Hyperparameters:

    Abstract: We develop a method for training small-scale (under 100M parameter) neural information retrieval models with as few as 10 gold relevance labels. The method depends on generating synthetic queries for documents using a language model (LM), and the key step is that we automatically optimize the LM prompt that is used to generate these queries based on training quality. In experiments with the BIRCO benchmark, we find that models trained with our method outperform RankZephyr and are competitive with RankLLama, both of which are 7B parameter models trained on over 100K labels. These findings point to the power of automatic prompt optimization for synthetic dataset generation. Paper

  3. Multilingual Recursive Summarization Trees:

    Implemented and evaluated MuRST for multilingual question answering, using recursive summarization trees and hard clustering, achieving a 3-8% improvement in F1 and BLEU scores across languages. Developed custom clustering algorithms for hierarchical summarization, enhancing QA accuracy for long-form documents. Poster, Paper

  4. Afaan Oromoo Language Processing:

    Conducted a comprehensive survey of around 100 native speakers to build a detailed dataset of common error types. Developed a spell-checker and autofill model for Afaan Oromoo.
